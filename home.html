  1. In 2020, children have been watching more YouTube videos than ever, reaching an average of 39 minutes a day.
    From babies to more grown up kids, this is their main form of visual entertainment. But do we know what are they looking at?

  2. Since young watchers are so many and naturally curious, some creators try to exploit their favourite cartoons characters and manage to reach them with inappropriate contents, by tricking YouTube’s system. What better way to gain views and money?
    This phenomenon, born in 2014 and still ongoing, is called ElsaGate.

    You are about to watch an experimental web series about YouTube’s inappropriate contents directed to kids. Each one of the 4 Episodes is dedicated to a popular cartoon character and it is composed by a selection of frames taken from different videos, actually collected from the platform.
